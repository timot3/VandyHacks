    What is GANdraw?

    We've created a website allowing users to easily interact with our trained Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) in order to create computer generated doodles.

    Before VandyHacks, we thought of hackathons as a place to practice our technical programming skills and try out something new like Machine Learning (ML). When considering the art theme of VandyHacks, we wanted to once again do something with ML to further our knowledge in the field, but this time with a more creative aspect.

    What is a GAN?

    A Generative Adversarial Network, or GAN, is a class of deep learning that consists of two networks competing against each other. The generator learns to create fake data that appears like real data to the discriminator, while the discriminator tries to determine whether a given piece of input data is real. The generator incorporates this feedback from the discriminator to make images more similar to the original dataset. In our case, the generator creates fake images that appear to be accurate depictions of a certain word to the discriminator.

    How was it built?

    We used a mixture of Python, Flask, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Google Cloud for training and hosting.

    What were some of the challenges you ran into?

    Training large amounts of data

    What were some of the main takeaways from this project?

    One of our main takeaways from this experience was learning how to train GANs on large datasets using Google Cloud.

    What is next for GANdraw?

    Due to time constraints and limited computing resources, we were only able to train our network on a small subset of the full dataset. Additionally, some features we were planning to implement include animating the resulting drawing and possibly running image analysis models to predict colors based on the word.
